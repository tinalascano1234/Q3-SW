Movie Reviews by Tina!

The Notebook

Details: The note The Notebook was directed by Nick Cassavetes and was released on September 15, 2004.

SYNOPSIS: In 1940s South Carolina, mill worker Noah Calhoun (Ryan Gosling) and rich girl Allie (Rachel McAdams) are desperately in love. But her parents don't approve. When Noah goes off to serve in World War II, it seems to mark the end of their love affair. In the interim, Allie becomes involved with another man (James Marsden). But when Noah returns to their small town years later, on the cusp of Allie's marriage, it soon becomes clear that their romance is anything but over.

source: IMDb.com

My review on The Notebook !

One of my comfort movies! It shows how love can be confusing and how many sacrifices should be made. It shows all the challenges couples have to face, and it showed how they found each other again because they were meant for each other. The story is well written and has the best ending it could possibly have, for me the movie is a 4.5/5!

You are my best friend as well as my lover, and I do not know which side of you I enjoy the most. I treasure each side, just as I have treasured our life together." - Nicholas Sparks, The Notebook

Five Feet Apart

Details: Five Feet Apart was a book writted by Rachael Lippincott and the Film adaptation was directed by Justin Baldoni. It was released on March 15th of 2019.

SYNOPSIS: A pair of teenagers with cystic fibrosis meet in a hospital and fall in love, though their disease means they must avoid close physical contact. Seventeen-year-old Stella spends most of her time in the hospital as a cystic fibrosis patient.

Source: IMDb.com

My review on Five Feet Apart!

The movie Five Feet Apart was one of the first movies to make me cry FOR WEEKS! Everytime I thought of the movie my eyes would well up. The fact that two people could fall in love so fast, but at the same time, are forbidden to be with each other becase of a life and death situation was heartbreaking. Definitely a 5/5!

“I’m tired of living without really living. I’m tired of wanting things. We can’t have a lot of things. But we could have this.” ― Rachael Lippincott

“Life is too short to waste a second.” – Stella (Haley Lu Richardson)
